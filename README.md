## ⚙️ Configuring Your Phone

After you launch the application, you must connect it to your telephony provider. Most providers supply the following details. Keep these ready:

* SIP Server Address: The web address of your phone provider.
* Username: Your unique extension or account ID.
* Password: The security credentials for your account.
* Protocol: Choose the default setting unless your provider requests otherwise.

Enter these details into the settings screen. Click the save button. The icon should change to green, which indicates your phone is ready to make calls.

## 🎧 Using the Interface

The main window displays your keypad and your active lines. 

* To place a call: Type the number into the keypad and click the green phone icon.
* To receive a call: Click the answer button when your computer rings. 
* To record a call: Click the record button during any active conversation. The app saves the file to your computer.
* To transfer a call: Click the transfer icon and select the extension where you want to send the caller.

## 📁 Managing Contacts and Logs

Click on the contacts tab to view your saved numbers. You can add new entries by clicking the plus icon. Fill in the name and the phone number, then save the record. 

Click on the history tab to view your call logs. The app shows the date, time, and duration of every call. You can clear this list at any time if you prefer to keep your workspace clean.

## 🧩 Troubleshooting

Check these common issues if you encounter trouble during your calls:

* No audio: Check your Windows sound settings. Make sure your headset is the default input and output device.
* Connection errors: Verify your internet connection. Ensure your SIP credentials are correct. Remove any extra spaces when typing your password.
* Application lag: Close other programs that use high amounts of data. This allows the softphone to prioritize your voice traffic.

Contact your IT administrator if you require specific port settings for your office network. The software typically uses standard ports for voice traffic, but some firewalls might require updates.

## 🛡 Security and Privacy

Your calls remain private. The application stores your call logs and recordings locally on your computer. It does not send your data to external servers. You maintain control over your files at all times. Always use a strong, unique password for your SIP account to prevent unauthorized access to your phone lines.
